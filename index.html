Biography

Born on January 17, 1942, in Louisville, Kentucky, Muhammad Ali was one of the greatest boxers of all time. Known for his speed, charisma, and ability to fight, Ali became an iconic figure both inside and outside of the boxing ring.

Ali, originally named Cassius Clay, won the World Heavyweight title three times and became a cultural and political symbol during the Civil Rights movement. His quotes, fights, and activism have made him an enduring figure.
